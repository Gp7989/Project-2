Subhash Chandra Bose

"Give me blood, and I will give you freedom!"

Subhash Chandra Bose, affectionately called Netaji, was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement against British rule. Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, he was known for his unwavering patriotism, determination, and leadership.

Early Life

Subhash Chandra Bose was born into a well-off Bengali family. An excellent student, he pursued higher education in England, passing the Indian Civil Services examination. However, his love for his country led him to resign and join the freedom struggle.

Role in India's Freedom Struggle

Bose played a vital role in organizing the Indian National Army (INA) and collaborating with countries like Germany and Japan during World War II to fight against the British. His rallying cry of "Jai Hind" continues to inspire millions to this day.

Legacy

Netaji's contributions to India's independence remain unparalleled. Despite his mysterious disappearance in 1945, his ideas of bravery and self-sacrifice resonate through generations. India honors him as a hero and a symbol of resilience.
